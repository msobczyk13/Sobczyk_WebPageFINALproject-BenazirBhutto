Biography


Benazir Bhutto was the first women elected to lead a Muslim state. She served as Prime Minister of Pakistan. Benazir was the daughter of a former prime minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. Her family came from the Bhutto tribe of Sindhi and she was born in Karachi, Sindh. During her early life, she spoke both Urdu and English.

Her parents were opponents of the military dictatorship run by General Zia-ul-Ha and in the summer of 1979 they were placed under house arrest where they faced great difficulties. She was released from house arrest in 1984 which is when she began campaigning for a restoration of democracy. She made a speach at the European Parliament in Strasbourg warning of human rights abuses in Pakistan, shortly after her brother was murdered. Bhutto and her party won the largest percentage of seats and were able to form a coalition government.

She was sworn in as Prime Minister for the first time in 1988 at the age of 35. She was removed from office 20 months later for alleged corruption. She was referred to as "Iron Lady" for her uncompromising approach to negotiating with unions when she was prime minister. She pursued various free market economic policies, such as deregulation, flexible labour markets and privatisation. However, rising unemployment and inflation made her economic policies unpopular. She was assassinated on December 27, 2007 when she was campaigning for the PPF. Later she was named one of seven winners of the United Nations Prize in the Field of Human Rights.
